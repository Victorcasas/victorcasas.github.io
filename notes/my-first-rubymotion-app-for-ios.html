My first RubyMotion app for iOS

Nov 21 2014 ios development

Since I bought the iPhone 2G I've wanted to do an iOS app.

First, I read Building iPhone Apps with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript book. I'm sure this way is much better than years ago but I think that native apps has better user experience and I want to take all the advantages of the iOS Platform.

Years later decided to learn Objetive-C I tried to follow the iTunes course Developing iOS 7 Apps for iPhone and iPad, but Objetive-C isn't for me.

I'm developing Ruby on Rails projects from 2005 so when I've noticed about RubyMotion I was very excited because I really enjoy programming with Ruby. Also, Ruby has a very active community that develop awesome gems. So, last May I traveled to San Francisco to assit to #inspect 2014, the RubyMotion developers conference.

Two days of workshop with Laurent Sansonetti, Colin T.A. Gray and Norberto Ortigoza to learn and share with other devs. Then two days of great talks, good food, awesome people... Finally, three more days to walk around the city and take pictures with my film camera. It was a great experience!

In summer I only worked at mornings so I took some time to read about RubyMotion, watch pending screencasts from MotionInMotion and search for sample apps at github to read code, know what gems use...

Baldboy told me about a Domestika's course: Diseña una App deliciosa. This is a pretty 25€ course that shows the process of designing an iOS app. From choosing the right features to get a preview, through drawing prototypes in paper. This course has been a good introduction for me on how design an iOS app and use Sketch 3. The course is in Spanish.

I haven't worked with Sketch 3 before. It has cool features like Mirror that allows you preview your design in your iPhone/iPad.

Finished the course, it's time to work on my first RubyMotion iOS application and after think in some ideas I decided to do a simple application to know how much I everyday spend in coffee, buying food, dinning out, taxi, etc.

I'm writing about all I'm learning along the development process. Moreover, I'll try to publish all resources, RubyMotion code included.

In my next post I'll show how I design my app and some useful tools that help me.
